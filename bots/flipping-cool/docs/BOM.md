# Flipping Cool Rev B BOM Baseline

## Purpose

This BOM tracks the Rev B max-integration electrical target while preserving
the Rev A mechanical/off-board assumptions. Rev B moves control, regulation,
sensing, status, and hardware authority selection onto one PCB.

## Off-Board By Design

- Battery: GNB 2S 450 mAh HV 80C XT30 pack, `GNB4502S80AHV`.
- Drive motors: 4x Pololu 75:1 Micro Metal Gearmotor HPCB 6V, item `3064`.
- Wheels: 4x BBB 22 mm antweight wheels.
- Weapon servos: 2x Savox SH-0255MG+.
- Receiver: Matek ELRS-R24-P6 PWM receiver.
- Drive ESC: external WEKA dual brushed ESC.

## Power And Protection

- `VBAT_RAW` enters at `J1` and reaches `VBAT_SW` only through the removable
  positive link at `J2`.
- `VBAT_SW` feeds WEKA power, the 6 V buck stage, the fused 3.3 V buck input,
  TVS clamp `D1`, bulk capacitance, and measurement points.
- `SERVO_6V` is generated on-board and powers the Matek receiver and servo
  outputs.
- `RX_6V` is a filtered branch of `SERVO_6V` for receiver power.
- `AUX_3V3` is generated by a dedicated buck and powers the MCU, muxes, status,
  I2C, and sensing network.
- `R40`-`R45` implement 100 k / 47 k rail dividers for `ADC_VBAT`,
  `ADC_SERVO_6V`, and `ADC_RX_6V`.
- `R46`/`R47` are DNP 4.7 k I2C pull-ups unless the sensor stack needs them.

## Control Contract

- `SAFE`: outputs must be forced safe by hardware/firmware policy before bench
  load testing.
- `RX_DIRECT`: receiver PWM remains the hardware-priority path and must work
  with the MCU unpowered or failed.
- `MCU_CONTROL`: ESP32-S3 may command PWM outputs and BLE alternate control,
  but only when the physical switch selects this state.
